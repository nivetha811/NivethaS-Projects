J. Jayalalithaa

Former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu

Jayalalithaa was an Indian politician and actress who served as Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu.

Here's the timeline of Dr. J.Jayalalithaa..

  • 1991- On 24 June, she became the youngest ever and the second female Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu. She held the position till May 12, 1996.
  • 1998- The general election of the Lok Sabha saw the AIADMK and its alliance securing 30 out of 40 seats.
  • 2001- On 14 May, Jayalalithaa became the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu for the second time and held the post till September 21, 2001.
  • 2002- In February, she was elected from the constituency of Andipatti.
  • 2002-2006 She remained the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu from March 2 to May 12,.
  • 2015- On May 11, the Karnataka High Court acquitted Jayalalithaa in the Disproportionate Assets case.
  • 2015- J Jayalalitha was sworn-in as the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu for a record-equalling fifth time on May 23.
  • 2016- On April 25 she filed her nomination papers in RK Nagar ahead of the Tamil Nadu Assembly elections.
  • 1989 She created a history of sorts by becoming the first chief minister to return to power for a second consecutive term.
  • 2016- She took oath as the chief minister of Tamil Nadu for a record sixth time on May 23.
  • 1972- The Government of Tamil Nadu honoured her with the Kalaimamani Award.
  • 1991- A degree of Doctor of Literature (LittD) was conferred upon her by the University of Madras.
  • 2011- Resolution was passed by the New Jersey General Assembly to appreciate her exemplary excellence and dedication as a leader and in service to the people of Tamil Nadu.
